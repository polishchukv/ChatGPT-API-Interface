- **Type 1 Bar (Inside Bar):**

  - Visualization: The high and low of the current bar are within the high and low of the preceding bar.

- **Type 2 Bar (Directional Bar):**
  - Definition: A bar that takes out one side of the previous bar's range (either the high or the low) but not both.

    - **2 Up (2U):** Takes out the previous high but not the low.
    - **2 Down (2D):** Takes out the previous low but not the high.
  - Significance: Shows a continuation in the direction of the breakout (up or down).

- **Type 3 Bar (Outside Bar):**
  - Definition: A bar that takes out both the high and the low of the previous bar.
  - Significance: Indicates increased volatility and potential reversal or continuation, capturing a broader price range.
  - Visualization: The high is higher and the low is lower than the preceding bar.
